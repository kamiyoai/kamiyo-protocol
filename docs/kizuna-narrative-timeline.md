# Kizuna Narrative Timeline

## Purpose
Give one clear non-technical story of what Kizuna is, why it matters, and how it rolls out.

## Core Message
Kizuna is KAMIYO's payment layer for AI agents: instant execution with built-in financial safety controls.

## Audience by Phase
1. Buyers: API platforms, infrastructure operators, fintech teams.
2. Builders: integrators, partner teams, protocol developers.
3. Ecosystem: wallets, chain teams, liquidity and data partners.

## Story Arc
## Phase 1: Problem (Weeks 1-2)
1. Agents can complete tasks, but payments are unreliable and risky.
2. Current options are either too slow or too exposed.

Proof points:
1. Failure patterns in agent-to-API payments.
2. Why unsecured payout risk blocks adoption.

## Phase 2: Solution (Weeks 3-4)
1. Introduce Kizuna as one payment rail with two controlled lanes.
2. Explain lane split in plain terms:
   - enterprise: pre-funded spending controls
   - crypto-fast: collateral-backed speed

Proof points:
1. Approval and settlement flow visual.
2. Guardrail examples that non-technical users can understand.

## Phase 3: Trust (Weeks 5-7)
1. Explain open rails vs hosted decision core.
2. Show replay protection and exactly-once settlement accounting.

Proof points:
1. Uptime and failure handling summaries.
2. Auditable event and reconciliation examples.

## Phase 4: Commercial Readiness (Weeks 8-10)
1. Show integration speed and operating simplicity.
2. Show pricing model and deployment readiness.

Proof points:
1. Time-to-integration benchmarks.
2. Early production metrics.

## Phase 5: Launch and Expansion (Weeks 11-12)
1. Position Kizuna as KAMIYO's core commercial product.
2. Transition from pilot stories to adoption stories.

Proof points:
1. Customer outcomes.
2. Partner ecosystem growth.

## Message Constraints
1. No internal field names, endpoint syntax, or protocol jargon in public posts.
2. No references to fallback internals or temporary implementation flags.
3. Keep language outcome-first: reliability, speed, control, safety.
4. Every post must answer:
   - what problem does this solve?
   - who is it for?
   - why is this safer or better now?
